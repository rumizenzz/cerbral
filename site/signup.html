No surprise bills, ever. Every tier has a hard monthly cap by default — if you hit it, Cerbral Cloud stops charging compute until next month, full stop. Metered overage is strictly opt-in and capped at whatever monthly ceiling you set. In the app you'll see a progress bar and a plain-English estimate ("about 180 more sessions this month at your current pace") — not a token counter. You should never have to do mental math about tokens to use Cerbral.

Why the numbers hold up. Cerbral Cloud routes by default to open-weights models (Qwen3-class) hosted on Galactuz-managed GPUs, where token cost lands around $0.90 per million blended — so $20 Pro's 5M token budget costs us ~$4.50, leaving room for payment processing and margin. The Boost credit pool is what lets users reach Claude Sonnet or GPT-Pro on demand; those calls debit the pool at provider cost, so heavy frontier use doesn't torch the margin on the base sub. Storage is the cheap part: at Cloudflare R2 rates ($0.015/GB/mo), the 10 / 100 / 500 GB quotas cost us $0.15, $1.50, and $7.50 per user per month — negligible next to compute. Same framing Ollama uses for Turbo, with a Cursor-style pay-as-you-go on top. Your conversations and memories stay in your GitHub brain-repo; Galactuz never sees the contents.

Frequently asked questions

What is Cerbral and how does it work?

Cerbral is a personal AI that runs on your own machine (via local Ollama) and stores everything it learns in your private GitHub repo. We never see your conversations. You sign in to cerbral.com just so your desktop app can find your brain repo — only your email, GitHub handle, and brain-repo URL land in our database.

What should I use Cerbral for?

Anything where you want an AI that remembers — your day-to-day thinking, long-running projects, coding with context, research, task agents. The difference from a cloud AI is that the memory sticks with you, not with them.

How much does it cost?

The AI is free. You run inference on your own machine, so there's no metering and no per-token bill — just electricity. Cerbral Cloud is a separate, optional subscription if you ever want extra compute or storage on top; pricing lives on the plans section.

Is my data really private?

Yes. Your conversations, memories, and sessions live in your own private GitHub repo on your GitHub account. Cerbral's own database stores only your email, GitHub handle, the URL to your brain repo, and a payment method if you subscribe to Cerbral Cloud — nothing else.
